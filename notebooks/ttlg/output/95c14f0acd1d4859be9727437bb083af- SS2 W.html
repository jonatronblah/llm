    A usefull fact is that Laser Rapier does 11/15 damange, while the Shard does 12/27 damage. The second number being the damage on overhand and the first on normal attack. This is due to a bug in the game, which makes the shard do 12 and 15 damage on overhand, instead of just doing 15. Psi sword does 18/24 in comparison. Rapier also does 1/2 damange vs annelids as its an energy weapon, and the shard does 1/2 vs robots.

    So if you've got overhand attack OS upgrade, use the shard!

    This thread reminds me of the hellishly-complicated FreeSpace 2 data list I did for my new game.

    I pored over the FS2 tablefiles for HOURS to extract the useful data, with the ultimate intention of making a new game out of it, along the lines of Battlefleet Gothic. That was months ago. The first incarnation required (no exaggeration) a bucket full of dice. I gave up on the project for some time, but it's back on my To Do list. In fact, it's on my Done list.

    FreeSpace 2: Battlefleet was finished at 8:00pm last night. It now uses ONE D20 instead of several hundred D6. The ship datasheets are vastly simplified, along with every rule my ingenuity could contrive. It is now playable within a mere day.

    Still to do:
    Sort out ship points values
    Sort out ship RP prices for campaign
    Do campaign rules
    Make it more interesting

    There is a 5Mb spreadsheet squatting on my hard drive with all the raw data I used for the game, including ship armour, ship dimensions, turret fire arcs, weapon damages, weapon ammo levels, Beam Cannon stats, and more. Out of the whole game, possibly the rules for Slash Beams gave me the most grief, but it's all turned out quite well.

    I dunno how I'd go about doing a System Shock RPG game, but I'd probably base it around Advanced D&D rules. If no one else is doin that, I might give it a go.

    hmm has anyone else used their grenade launcher with heavy 6 and level 2 modification only to find that frag grenades seem better than disruption? well looking through the shock2.gam with shocked it seems that disruption grenades are broken, and don't proagte the source scale through to the explosion like all the other grenades. This means that it basiaclly ignores any heavy skill or modifications or OS upgrades and just does a measily 35 damage, where as you can get up to a * 5.39 multiplier with frag grenades, doing a total of 107.8 damange. This can be fixed by swaping the green core and disrtuption burst explosion fx links around quite easily. Proximity grenades also seem to not benefit from upgrades, but that is a lot harder to fix as its all scripted to do the whole proximity detonation thingy.
