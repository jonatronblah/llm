    SS2 Impressions from Some Idiot You Don't Know

    Hey all,

    I'm new here. (As if you couldn't tell.) I'd just like to say that System Shock 2 has to be the scariest game I've ever played. Resident Evil has nothing on it. (This is also because the RE series, well, sucks, but that's a different discussion.) Granted, as I've progressed, it's gotten less freaky, but it's still wholly disturbing. It's really hard not to get chills the first time you hear a Hybrid scream "I'm sorrrryyyy..." or "Killll meeee..." I've jumped a good four or five times after hearing something behind me, then turning around and finding a Pipe Hybrid breathing down my collar.

    The sound and ambience is simply wonderful, and the graphics can't be beaten. I'm blessed to be able to play it in 1024x768; everything is so smooth and detailed! Also, the character customization is a welcome innovation to the FPS genre (which definately has more than its share of awful, lackluster World War 2 games and boring Sci-Fi shooters). Following the advice of the strategy guide, I'm roleplaying along with it. It's all in my mind, of course, but the game is much more fun imagining that I'm a maintenance man from hell, refusing to use guns unless necessary, instead relying on melee weapons and my keen hacking and repairing skills. (I also have an addiction to junk food, downing any and all cans of cola I can find, even those salvaged from zombies with shotguns.)

    I guess all I'm really trying to say is, System Shock 2 is one of the best first-person shooters, ever. Hands down. Yes, it beats Half-Life. It doesn't just beat it, it hangs it up on a nail on the wall and taunts it like a schoolyard bully. The mood, plot, and feel of SS2 is simply one of the greatest ever. I'll stop slobbering now, though.

    Yes, I take back what I said about the graphics. I installed Rebirth last night.

    Wow.

    Remember what I said about how the game seemed to lose some of its fright? After installing SS2: Rebirth, I'm living in fear of the hybrids once more. Those things are ugly. (In a good way, of course!) Also, I got chills the first time I saw one of the updated crew corpses, with his twisted face made more lifelike by the high resolution model update and his wounds more detailed and gruesome. I don't usually go for the shock factor in games -- if all you can do to scare me is throw a bunch of gory stiffs at me, you're not trying hard enough -- but SS2 puts emotion into it. It's really something, seeing a ghost, then finding their mangled body nearby. The game has the uncanny ability to frighten by inadvertantly using the player's imagination against him. Just what did she see? What just shot her? Eww, how did THAT happen?
